Eye-Tracking Driven Dyslexia Detection
paper ↗

Co-authored a research paper proposing a data-efficient machine learning approach to detect developmental dyslexia using eye-tracking biomarkers. Addressed data scarcity by synthetically augmenting the ETDD70 dataset to 210 participants . Engineered spatial and temporal features (e.g., fixation durations, saccade lengths) to train an XGBoost classifier, achieving a peak test accuracy of 92.86% and a train-test gap of only 0.0178 .
